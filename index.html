About Me

About Me

I'm a full stack web developer and recent graduate of Coding Bootcamp at UT Austin, hosted by Trilogy Education. Trilogy's Chief Learning Officer looked over my Github account and asked if I wanted to tutor bootcamp students. I enjoy teaching - I used to teach the LSAT for Princeton Review - so I started online tutoring late last year. Soon I'll be running Trilogy's first in-person tutoring and I plan on acting as TA for an upcoming class.

I'm also dedicated to becoming the best programmer I can be, and in that spirit, I have become an avid student of algorithms and data structures. I have completed three of four parts of the Stanford Algorithms specialization on Coursera, covering the full material of Stanford's own algorithms course. I also completed Part I of Princeton's Algorithms Coursera course, which was a great opportunity to practice coding in Java.

Prior to the bootcamp, I ran a small audio/video business with a focus on control systems. I installed and programmed touchscreen and mobile app-based interfaces for controlling A/V, security, and other electronic systems. I also worked on 'drivers' to interface with new components over RS232 and IP via existing APIs.

I tried my hand at game development years ago when Microsoft's XNA was the 'hot' engine for cross-platform XBox360 and PC development. C# only took a week to get up and running with, as I learned C++ as part of my high school AP CompSci class. Unlike Unity and other engines today, XNA has no native collision detection, so I wrote my own box model for floors/walls and per-pixel detection for projectiles. Microsoft soon abandoned XNA entirely and I moved on, but with the XNA Redistributable it's still possible to run the demo level.

Please check out my projects below. Thanks for visiting!
